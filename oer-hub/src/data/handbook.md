# Open 4 Peer Review (O4PR) — Reviewer Handbook

## About This Project

The Open 4 Peer Review (O4PR) project strengthens the quality of open educational resources (OER) through structured, constructive peer review. The platform supports six rubric areas: Accessibility, Copyright, Copy Editing, Disciplinary Appropriateness, eLearning, and Universal Design for Learning (UDL).

The goal is not to evaluate or judge OER creators — it is to support a collaborative process that helps authors and reviewers work together to strengthen openly licensed learning materials.

---

## How the Single-Point Rubric Works

The rubrics use a **single-point format**. The central column describes the established standard of quality. Reviewers provide feedback in two directions:

- **Exceeds the standard** — aspects that demonstrate particularly strong alignment or notable strengths.
- **Needs improvement** — aspects that do not yet meet the standard or could be strengthened through revision.

This structure shifts the focus from assigning scores to providing **descriptive, evidence-based feedback**.

---

## Providing Effective Feedback: Core Principles

### 1. Focus on the Standard
- Respond directly to the criterion being reviewed
- Focus on how the OER aligns with the standard
- Avoid unrelated personal preferences

**Sentence starters:**
- "This resource aligns with the standard by…"
- "An area where this meets the criterion is…"
- "This could more fully align with the standard by…"
- "In relation to this criterion, I noticed…"

### 2. Be Evidence-Based
- Point to exact elements (text, images, structure, activities)
- Describe what you observe
- Avoid general statements without explanation

**Sentence starters:**
- "In this section, the resource shows…"
- "For example, the image/text/activity…"
- "A specific example of this is…"
- "This part of the resource demonstrates…"

### 3. Highlight Strengths and Opportunities
- Identify where the OER meets or exceeds the standard
- Note strengths that should be retained or built upon
- Suggest areas for improvement

**Sentence starters:**
- "A strength of this resource is…"
- "This effectively supports learners by…"
- "One area that could be strengthened is…"
- "An opportunity for improvement is…"

### 4. Provide Actionable Suggestions
- Offer clear, practical suggestions
- Focus on feasible and meaningful changes
- Avoid vague recommendations

**Sentence starters:**
- "Consider adding…"
- "This could be improved by…"
- "One possible revision would be…"
- "To strengthen this, you might…"

### 5. Use a Constructive and Respectful Tone
- Use respectful, professional language
- Focus on the resource, not the creator
- Frame comments as opportunities

**Sentence starters:**
- "To support learner understanding, you might…"
- "This section could be clarified by…"
- "You might consider revising this by…"

### 6. Do NOT Assign Scores or Make Judgments
The rubrics are not designed to assign grades or determine whether an OER should be used. Focus on alignment and improvement only.

### 7. Keep the Learner in Mind
- How learners interact with the material
- Where they may encounter barriers
- What supports understanding and engagement

**Sentence starters:**
- "From a learner perspective, this may…"
- "Learners might find this section…"
- "This could support learners by…"

### 8. Embrace a Continuous Improvement Mindset
OER are living resources that evolve over time. Peer review is part of an ongoing process of refinement, not a gatekeeping mechanism.

## Rubric Standards Reference

### Accessibility
Ensures all learners — regardless of ability, technology, or learning context — can perceive, navigate, and engage with course materials.

- **C1 Text Structure & Screen Reader Navigation** — Proper heading hierarchy (H1-H6), logical nesting, correct list markup, reading order matches visual layout.
- **C2 Visual Design & Color** — WCAG 2.1 AA contrast ratios (4.5:1 normal text, 3:1 large text), no information conveyed by color alone, minimum 12pt body text.
- **C3 Alternative Text & Image Accessibility** — All informative images have descriptive alt text, decorative images marked null, charts include data tables or descriptions.
- **C4 Multimedia Accessibility** — Videos have accurate closed captions and transcripts, audio has transcripts, media players are keyboard accessible.
- **C5 Interactive Elements & Forms** — All interactive elements keyboard accessible, form fields have labels, error messages are descriptive, focus indicators visible.
- **C6 Table Structure & Data Presentation** — Data tables have proper header markup and scope attributes, no tables used for layout, complex tables have summaries.
- **C7 Link Quality & Context** — Link text is descriptive and meaningful out of context, links indicate when opening in new windows, purpose is clear.
- **C8 Technical Format & Compatibility** — Content available in formats that preserve accessibility, works across multiple devices and platforms.

### Copyright
Ensures materials can be legally used, adapted, and shared with clear and accurate licensing.

- **C1 Original Content Licensing** — All original content clearly licensed under an open license (CC BY, CC BY-SA, etc.), prominently displayed, applied consistently.
- **C2 Third-Party Content Documentation** — All third-party content identified, source noted with sufficient detail, legal basis for inclusion clearly indicated.
- **C3 Attribution Practices** — Proper attribution for all third-party content: creator name, title, source/URL, license type, modifications. Placed near content or in credits.
- **C4 Fair Use Application** — Copyrighted materials incorporated under fair use align with fair use principles (purpose, nature, amount, market effect), documented appropriately.
- **C5 License Compatibility** — All openly licensed materials comply with their license terms, compatible licenses when combining materials.
- **C6 Public Domain Materials** — Public domain materials accurately categorized, clearly communicated to users, status documented.
- **C7 Copyright Status Communication** — Each component clearly communicates copyright status and reuse permissions; distinguishes original, openly licensed, fair use, and public domain.
- **C8 Permissions & Documentation Trail** — For materials used with explicit permission, documentation is maintained including scope, attribution requirements, and restrictions.

### Copy Editing
Ensures materials are clear, consistent, and free of errors.

- **C1 Grammar & Syntax** — Correct subject-verb agreement, pronoun-antecedent agreement, appropriate verb tenses, complete sentences.
- **C2 Spelling & Typographical Accuracy** — Correct spelling per chosen language variant, technical terms spelled accurately, fewer than one typo per 5,000 words.
- **C3 Punctuation & Mechanics** — Follows style guide conventions consistently for commas, semicolons, apostrophes, capitalization, number usage.
- **C4 Style Guide Consistency** — A recognized editorial style guide (APA, Chicago, MLA) applied consistently throughout.
- **C5 Internal Consistency & Usage** — Terminology used consistently, commonly confused words used correctly, parallel structure maintained, consistent point of view.
- **C6 Clarity & Readability** — Clear, concise sentences accessible to intended audience, jargon defined when first introduced, smooth transitions, no ambiguity.
- **C7 Formatting & Visual Consistency** — Logical heading hierarchy, consistent font usage, consistent lists and spacing, professional visual presentation.
- **C8 Citations, References & Attributions** — In-text citations follow style guide, reference lists complete and accurate, all borrowed material cited, attribution statements present.
- **C9 Cross-References & Navigational Elements** — Internal cross-references accurate and functional, hyperlinks work correctly, figure and table numbers sequential.
- **C10 Inclusive & Accessible Language** — Avoids bias related to gender, race, disability, age, sexual orientation; culturally sensitive examples; supports multilingual audiences.

### Disciplinary Appropriateness
Ensures OER align with expectations, practices, and ways of knowing within a specific field.

- **C1 Content Accuracy & Validity** — Factually accurate, reflects current disciplinary understanding, disputed content identified, sources credible and verified.
- **C2 Currency & Contemporaneity** — Incorporates recent scholarship, aware of evolving perspectives, reflects current state of field.
- **C3 Disciplinary Completeness & Coverage** — Covers core content thoroughly, addresses essential topics at appropriate depth, explicit connections between concepts.
- **C4 Scholarly Rigor & Theoretical Framework** — Strong scholarly approach, appropriate theoretical grounding, evidence-based reasoning.
- **C5 College-Level Appropriateness & Cognitive Demand** — Appropriate intellectual complexity, encourages critical thinking and higher-order reasoning, includes scaffolding for complex topics.
- **C6 Source Quality & Documentation** — High-quality credible sources, consistent and accurate citation practices, documentation supports claims.
- **C7 Assessment & Practice Quality** — Well-aligned assessments, appropriate level of difficulty, variety of exercise types, most important learning expectations targeted.

### eLearning
Focuses on how OER are structured to support learning through clear, engaging, and goal-aligned design.

- **C1 Usability & Technical Functionality** — Intuitive interface, logical navigation, works across major OS/browsers, scalable for any class size.
- **C2 Technical Support & Documentation** — Comprehensive support available through multiple channels, clear documentation, training resources for instructors and students.
- **C3 Mobile Accessibility & Cross-Platform** — Fully accessible on mobile, minimal functional difference from desktop, works across iOS and Android.
- **C4 LMS Integration & Interoperability** — Can be embedded in LMS, LTI compliance, grade passback if applicable, single sign-on.
- **C5 Data Privacy, Security & Ownership** — Adheres to FERPA/GDPR, privacy policies clearly stated, users own their data, content not locked into proprietary systems.
- **C6 Cost, Sustainability & Resource Requirements** — Essential features free or costs clearly disclosed, no hidden fees, stable organization with evidence of sustainability.
- **C8 Accessibility** — Meets WCAG 2.1 AA, works with screen readers and keyboard navigation, videos captioned, no learners excluded due to accessibility barriers.
- **C9 Pedagogical Effectiveness** — Supports intended learning objectives, aligns with sound pedagogical principles, facilitates higher-order thinking, provides actionable feedback.
- **C10 Learning Analytics & Customization** — Collects meaningful engagement data, user-friendly analytics, instructors can customize for their course context.

### Universal Design for Learning (UDL)
Focuses on designing flexible learning experiences responsive to diverse learner needs.

- **C1 Multiple Options for Representation & Perception** — Content presented in multiple formats/modalities, key vocabulary clarified, complex ideas have both linguistic and non-linguistic representations.
- **C2 Learner Choice in Expression & Demonstration** — Learning goals separated from means of achieving them, meaningful options for expression, assessment formats allow varied modes.
- **C3 Engagement, Relevance & Cultural Responsiveness** — Connects to real-world applications, represents diverse identities and cultural frameworks authentically, avoids stereotypical representations.
- **C4 Transparent Expectations, Models & Actionable Feedback** — Learning objectives explicitly stated, success criteria provided in advance, models/exemplars included, formative feedback emphasizes process.
- **C5 Collaboration, Communication & Community** — Options for individual and collaborative work, collaborative activities have clear structures, multiple communication formats supported.
- **C6 Metacognition, Self-Regulation & Strategic Learning** — Supports goal-setting and progress monitoring, opportunities for self/peer assessment, prompts for metacognitive awareness.
- **C7 Equity, Bias Awareness & Inclusive Environments** — Addresses potential bias and stereotype threat, strategies to reduce anxiety, fosters discussion of diverse perspectives.
- **C8 OER-Specific UDL Considerations** — Leverages open licensing for adaptation, available in multiple accessible formats, metadata describes UDL features and customization possibilities.

## Calibration Reminder

Because peer review often involves multiple reviewers, the rubrics support **shared interpretation** of quality criteria. Practicing with the rubrics helps reviewers develop consistency in how they interpret standards and identify evidence within learning materials.

Over time, this shared understanding helps institutions and communities build trust in the quality of openly licensed resources.
