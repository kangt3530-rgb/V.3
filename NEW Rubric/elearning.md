# eLearning Review

eLearning design focuses on how OER are structured to support learning, guiding learners through content in ways that are clear, engaging, and aligned with learning goals. High-quality OER are intentionally organized, helping learners understand what they are expected to learn and how to navigate the material effectively.

In the context of OER, strong eLearning design is critical because resources are often used in different courses, modalities, and teaching approaches. Without intentional design, even accurate content may be difficult for learners to engage with or apply. Well-designed OER support active learning, provide clear organization, and include opportunities for practice and feedback.

This area includes elements such as alignment between learning objectives and content, logical organization, and the inclusion of instructional supports that guide learners through the material. The criteria in this section are designed to support constructive, improvement-focused feedback, helping reviewers identify ways to strengthen the learning experience.

---

## C1: Usability and Technical Functionality

1. eLearning has an intuitive, user-friendly interface that instructors and students can learn to use effectively with minimal training.
2. Navigation is logical and consistent.
3. eLearning functions reliably across major desktop and mobile operating systems and current web browsers without requiring proprietary software or outdated plugins.
4. eLearning can be scaled to accommodate classes of any size without performance degradation.
5. Core features function consistently and reliably for all users.

## C2: Technical Support and Documentation

1. Comprehensive technical support is readily available through multiple channels (email, chat, phone, knowledge base).
2. Documentation is clear, complete, and addresses common questions and troubleshooting scenarios effectively.
3. Training resources are available for both instructors and students.
4. Response time for support inquiries is reasonable.
5. Help documentation and tutorials are readily accessible within the tool.

## C3: Mobile Accessibility and Cross-Platform Documentation

1. eLearning is fully accessible on mobile devices through either a dedicated app or mobile-optimized browser version.
2. Mobile design appropriately addresses constraints of smaller screens while maintaining core functionality.
3. There is minimal functional difference between mobile and desktop versions.
4. eLearning functions effectively across both iOS and Android operating systems.
5. Users can access essential features regardless of the device, operating system, or browser they choose.

## C4: Learning Management System (LMS) Integration and Interoperability

1. eLearning can be embedded or fully integrated into LMS while maintaining full functionality.
2. Integration is achieved through standard protocols (LTI compliance) that preserve user experience.
3. Grade data, if applicable, passes back to the LMS gradebook seamlessly.
4. Single sign-on authentication works properly.
5. The tool does not require students to create separate external accounts when accessed through the LMS.

## C5: Data Privacy, Security, and Ownership

1. eLearning adheres to data privacy regulations and institutional policies (FERPA, GDPR as applicable).
2. Privacy policies are clearly stated and easily accessible.
3. Users maintain ownership of their intellectual property and data.
4. If external accounts are required, eLearning has been vetted for data protection compliance.
5. Users can save, archive, export, and download their work in multiple standard formats.
6. Content is not locked into proprietary systems.
7. Student data is not shared with third parties for commercial purposes.

## C6: Cost, Sustainability, and Resource Requirements

1. All essential features of eLearning are available free of charge for educational use, or costs are clearly disclosed upfront and are reasonable.
2. If fees are required, they are unlikely to create financial barriers for students.
3. eLearning is supported by a stable organization with evidence of long-term sustainability.
4. Proper use does not require specialized equipment beyond what is typically available to students (computer, internet connection, standard peripherals).
5. There are no hidden costs or required upgrades that limit functionality.

## C8: Accessibility

1. eLearning meets accessibility guidelines (WCAG 2.1 AA standards, Section 508 requirements).
2. The interface works effectively with assistive technologies including screen readers and keyboard navigation.
3. Color contrast meets accessibility standards.
4. Alternative text is provided for images.
5. Videos include captions.
6. eLearning is designed to address needs of diverse users with varying abilities and learning preferences.
7. No learners are prevented from participating due to accessibility barriers.

## C9: Pedagogical Effectiveness and Learning Enhancement

1. eLearning demonstrably supports and enhances the intended learning objectives of the OER.
2. Features align with sound pedagogical principles and effective instructional strategies.
3. eLearning adds meaningful value beyond what could be achieved through simpler means.
4. eLearning facilitates higher-order thinking skills appropriate to the content and level.
5. eLearning actively engages students through interactive, meaningful activities.
6. Students receive timely, actionable feedback on their performance.

## C10: Learning Analytics and Customization

1. eLearning collects meaningful data on student engagement, performance, and progress.
2. Analytics are presented in a user-friendly format that instructors can easily interpret and use for instructional decision-making.
3. Data can be accessed at both individual and aggregate levels.
4. Instructors can easily customize eLearning to suit their specific course context, learning outcomes, and teaching approaches.
5. eLearning can be adapted to different pedagogical strategies.
6. Course management features are efficient and do not create excessive administrative burden.
