# Disciplinary Appropriateness

Disciplinary appropriateness ensures that OER align with the expectations, practices, and ways of knowing within a specific field of study. High-quality OER reflect accurate content, appropriate terminology, and relevant examples that support meaningful learning within the discipline.

This dimension is particularly important in OER because resources are often adapted for use in varied courses and contexts. Without careful attention to disciplinary alignment, materials may lack the depth, accuracy, or context needed to effectively support student learning. High-quality OER demonstrate credibility and relevance, enabling faculty to confidently integrate them into their courses.

In practice, disciplinary appropriateness includes accuracy of content, alignment with current knowledge or standards in the field, and the use of examples or applications that are meaningful to learners. The criteria in this section provide guidance for evaluating how well an OER supports learning within a specific discipline and are intended to guide constructive, improvement-focused feedback.

---

## C1: Content Accuracy and Validity

1. All content is factually accurate with reliable, well-supported information.
2. Facts and data reflect current disciplinary understanding.
3. Any disputed content is identified as currently disputed or part of current debates.
4. Content is authoritative and appropriate for the intended college-level audience.
5. Sources are credible and properly verified.

## C2: Currency and Contemporaneity

1. Content incorporates recent scholarship/research and praxis with good attention to contemporary developments.
2. Shows awareness of evolving disciplinary perspectives while maintaining connection to foundational knowledge.
3. Information reflects current state of the field.

## C3: Disciplinary Completeness and Coverage

1. Covers core disciplinary content thoroughly with good integration of key concepts.
2. Addresses essential topics with appropriate depth for target audience.
3. Presents main ideas clearly with explicit connections between concepts.
4. Content connects important associated concepts within the subject matter rather than presenting topics in isolation.
5. Scope aligns with standard expectations for the discipline.

## C4: Scholarly Rigor and Theoretical Framework

1. Demonstrates strong scholarly approach with solid analytical depth and appropriate theoretical grounding.
2. Shows competent understanding of disciplinary methodology and theoretical frameworks.
3. Evidence-based reasoning supports arguments and conclusions.

## C5: College-Level Appropriateness and Cognitive Demand

1. Demonstrates appropriate intellectual complexity for college students.
2. Encourages critical thinking and higher-order reasoning with suitable cognitive demand for the discipline.
3. Content promotes academic skill development and independent inquiry.
4. Complex topics include sufficient scaffolding and direction.

## C6: Source Quality and Documentation

1. Uses high-quality, credible sources with consistent and accurate citation practices.
2. Shows good engagement with authoritative scholarship.
3. Documentation supports claims and enables verification.
4. Citation format follows disciplinary standards.

## C7: Assessment and Practice Quality

1. Provides well-aligned assessments that measure major concepts appropriately.
2. The level of difficulty results from the complexity of subject-area content and cognitive demand, not from unrelated issues (e.g., overly complex vocabulary in math word problems).
3. Assessment modes include sufficient variety of exercise types and formats to facilitate mastery of targeted skills.
4. Most important aspects of learning expectations are targeted and given appropriate weight.
